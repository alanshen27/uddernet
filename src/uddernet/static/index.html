UdderNet

Early mastitis detection for dairy herds — combining milk-sensor data and udder imagery in one screening flow.

What you'll do Enter milk measurements, upload an udder photo, and get a fused diagnosis from our two neural networks.
Swipe through This walkthrough takes about a minute. Tap Continue to begin.
📊

Step 1 — Sensor data

Our MastitisMLP reads seven milk measurements — temperature, pH, conductivity, somatic cell count, yield, clotting, and lactation day — and outputs a mastitis probability.

How it works A 3-layer feed-forward network trained on 800 labelled milk samples. Inputs are standardised; output is P(mastitis).
🥛

Step 2 — Udder image

Our ResNet-18 CNN classifies udder photographs. It was pretrained on ImageNet and fine-tuned on ~600 labelled images from the iGEN dataset.

How it works Images are resized to 224×224, passed through a convolutional backbone, and classified as positive (mastitis) or negative (healthy).

Step 3 — Fusion

Neither modality alone tells the full story. Late fusion blends both probabilities with image-led weighting:

Image says negative Trust the photo more → 70% image + 30% sensor
Image says positive Confirm with sensors → 30% image + 70% sensor

Tap Analyse on the next screen to run the combined model.
